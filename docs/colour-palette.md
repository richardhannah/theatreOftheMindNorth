# Fortune & Glory — Colour Palette

Gold-themed, dark-first colour palette for a D&D campaign website.

---

## Core Palette

| Name | Hex | CSS Variable | Role |
|---|---|---|---|
| Gold | `#F5C842` | `--gold` | Primary headings & key UI |
| Deep Gold | `#D4960A` | `--deep-gold` | Buttons & CTAs |
| Antique Gold | `#7A5C1A` | `--antique-gold` | Borders & dividers |
| Dungeon Black | `#1a1208` | `--dungeon-black` | Page background |
| Dark Parchment | `#2E1F06` | `--dark-parchment` | Elevated panels & cards |

| Worn Leather | `#5C4A2A` | `--worn-leather` | Secondary surfaces |

---

## Usage Guidelines

### Page Background
- **`#1a1208`** Dungeon Black — the dominant surface. Dark and immersive, like candlelight in a vault.
- Use **`#2E1F06`** Dark Parchment for cards, sidebars, and elevated panels.

### Primary Headings & Key UI
- **`#F5C842`** Gold — hero headings, site title, nav links on hover, and interactive highlights.
- Use sparingly so it reads as precious, not cheap.

### Body Text
- **`#F2E8C9`** Parchment — all running prose.
- Warmer than white; feels like ink on aged vellum. High contrast against dark backgrounds without the harshness of pure white.

### Buttons & CTAs
- **`#D4960A`** Deep Gold — primary buttons and call-to-action elements.
- Slightly darker than headline gold so it reads as an affordance, not just decoration.

### Borders & Dividers
- **`#7A5C1A`** Antique Gold — borders, card outlines, `<hr>` rules, table lines.
- Muted enough not to compete with `#F5C842`, rich enough to feel intentional.

### Accent & Danger
- **`#8B0000`** Blood Crimson — warnings, damage indicators, enemy tags, or dramatic pull-quotes.
- Never use for large text blocks.

### Secondary Surfaces
- **`#5C4A2A`** Worn Leather — secondary cards, tooltips, dropdown menus, and info panels.
- Warm mid-tone that sits between the dark background and the gold highlights.

### Secondary Text & Labels
- **`#E0C97A`** Pale Gold — subheadings, metadata, timestamps, and secondary labels.
- Less impactful than `#F5C842` but still on-brand. Good for stat blocks and table headers.

---

## Design Principles

**Stay disciplined with gold.** Use `#F5C842` only for the most important elements (site title, hero text, active nav). Overusing it kills the effect — scarcity is what makes gold feel valuable.

**Parchment, not white.** Use `#F2E8C9` for all body copy. Pure white feels cold and modern, breaking the immersion.

**Crimson as a spice.** `#8B0000` is powerful — a little goes a long way. Perfect for marking enemies, critical hits, or dramatic chapter titles. Don't use it for navigation or general UI.

**Typography tip:** A serif font (Palatino, EB Garamond, or Cinzel for display) pairs beautifully with this palette and reinforces the ancient-treasure aesthetic.
